Applied AI 层的四件套 / The Four Components of the Applied-AI Playbook

Levie 一条长 thread 立住 applied AI 层不是 LLM 之上的薄 wrapper:workflow bridge + model router + FDE 实施 + domain-specific GTM 四件套叠起来才是 playbook;bitter lesson 在极限处可能抹平这一切,但「企业今天就需要帮助转型」——这是今天最值得抄下来的判断。

AL
Aaron Levie
Box CEO
@levie
中文

Levie(Box CEO)这天写了最长的一条 open thread:applied-AI 层不是 LLM 之上的薄 wrapper。「一开始有人批评说这就是一层薄膜,现在看下来在企业里真正把 agentic workflow 跑起来,远比想象的复杂。复杂度在哪,护城河和价值就在哪积累。」

他拆出四件撑起 playbook 的事,单件都不够:(1)Build the workflow bridge——为任务捕捉数据、配套专属工具、设计 human-in-the-loop UX;「远远不止把 output tokens 摆出来」才是护城河成立的条件。(2)Act as model router——必要时用 frontier(GPT-5.5 / Fable),能用更便宜的闭源/开源就用便宜的;只有对所有模型都有深度 evals 的平台,才能在商业上对齐地做这件事。(3)Drive implementation via FDE——改 change management、清理数据、重写流程、对齐 SLA;这是按 domain 沉淀的能力,不会被商品化。(4)Domain-specific GTM——语言、安全合规姿态、行业活动、SI/咨询伙伴;通用 GTM 说不出口客户的语言,applied layer 的位就在这。最后一句:bitter lesson 在极限处确实可能把所有这些抹平,但「企业今天就需要帮助转型」——你现在交付的东西就是赢的东西,不管模型以后走到哪。

Levie 把 applied-AI 层立成四件套:workflow bridge(深于 output tokens 的数据/工具/UX) + model router(frontier vs 自训/开源的混合路由,需深度 evals) + FDE 驱动的实施与 change management + domain-specific GTM;bitter lesson 在极限处可能抹平这一切,但「企业今天就需要帮助转型」——现在交付的就是赢的。

English

Aaron Levie (Box CEO) writes the longest open thread of the day: the applied-AI layer is not a thin wrapper over the LLM. 'Despite some of the initial critique that this would just be a thin layer on the LLM, it's turning out that actually driving agentic workflows in an enterprise is far more complex. And anywhere there's complexity you generally gain a moat and value over time.'

He lays out four load-bearing components, each of which alone is insufficient: (1) Build the workflow bridge — features that capture the data, tools, and human-in-the-loop UX specific to the task; going 'far deeper than just presenting the output tokens' is the moat condition. (2) Act as model router — leverage frontier when needed (GPT-5.5 / Fable), efficient closed/open when not; only platforms with deep evals across all models can do this in a business-model-aligned way. (3) Drive implementation via FDE — change-management, data cleanup, process re-engineering, SLA work; this is per-domain expertise that doesn't commoditize. (4) Build domain-specific GTM — language, security/compliance posture, industry events, SI/consulting partners; generic GTM can't speak the customer's language. Closing note: 'the bitter lesson solves all of this in the limit' is possible, but 'enterprises need help changing today' — what you're shipping now is what wins, regardless of where models land.

The past couple months we may be witnessing what the Applied AI layer will look like at scale. Despite some of the initial critique that this would just be a thin layer on the LLM, it's turning out that actually driving agentic workflows in an enterprise is far more complex. And anywhere there's complexity you generally gain a moat and value over time. Here are a few of the components that appear to make up the playbook based on the examples we're collectively seeing in coding, legal, healthcare, customer support, financial services and other fields: * Build the features that bridge the gap between the intelligence and the workflow... * Act as the model router balancing frontier intelligence with cheaper models... * Drive the actual implementation and change management via FDE or equivalent... * Implement domain specific GTM that creates expertise in that field... A final note. There remains a view that a lot of this is all mitigated by model intelligence alone, and the bitter lesson solves all of this in the limit. That's possibly true, but enterprises need help changing *today*.
