Peter Yang 对「level 5」这套诚实披露框架的反驳:「这套框架有个小问题:如果你等到 level 5 才有把握跟别人说『这里出问题了』,而火已经烧了几天,你的『解决方案』其实根本没修好——你这时候的位置,比 level 1 就在喊『着火了』的那个人要糟糕得多。」 Yang 的意思是:框架听起来合理,但 timing 才是全部,等到你 level 5 才有把握,损害已经造成。

这一点比原来讨论的语境更通用:任何把沟通按「我有多 confident」排序的框架都是坏的,因为信息的成本主要在接收方,延迟的成本也主要在接收方。「level 1 喊一声『着火了,可能不大』」结构上就是比「level 5 出来说『我们解决了』但火已经烧完」好。Yang 在做的是替观众把这个 level-5 框架拆开,免得有人被它哄住。

There's just one small problem with this framework: If you wait till level 5 or whatever to tell other people that there's a problem when there's been a burning fire for days and your "solution" actually didn't fix the problem at all, you're 100% in a worse situation than someone who is level 1 and told people the fire was happening.

English

Peter Yang pushes back on the 「level 5」 framework for honest disclosure: 「There's just one small problem with this framework: if you wait till level 5 or whatever to tell other people that there's a problem when there's been a burning fire for days and your 'solution' actually didn't fix the problem at all, you're 100% in a worse situation than someone who is level 1 and told people the fire was happening.」 Yang is saying the framework sounds reasonable but the timing is the whole game - by the time you reach level 5 confidence, the damage is already done.

The point generalizes past the original context: any framework that ranks communication by 「how confident I feel」 is broken, because the cost of the message is mostly on the receiver side and the cost of the delay is mostly theirs too. A level-1 disclosure that says 「fire, may be small」 is structurally better than a level-5 disclosure that arrives after the fact. Yang is doing the editorial work of pulling the level-5 frame apart on behalf of the audience that would otherwise be lulled by it.
