AI & I by Every: 'We Automated Everything With AI and Tripled Our Headcount.' Brandon (Every COO) interviews Dan Shipper about his piece 'After Automation' published May 21.

The hook: 'You prompt AI to do something. It blows your mind. You feel inadequate. You feel like, oh my god. This thing's gonna take my job. The piece argues the further an agent gets from a human, the less valuable it is — and on the Fable-5 launch day of all days, 'After Automation' lands like a corrective: ride the models, lead an ambitious life, the value is in staying close to the loop.

Josh Woodward(Google Labs 产品负责人):「对软件的需求会爆表。」

Google Labs 这边对 Karpathy 那个 Jevons 悖论的呼应:软件生产被「水龙头化」之后,瓶颈从「能不能做」挪到「该不该做」。什么样的东西该做、什么不该做、什么该作为公共物品,这变成新问题。Woodward 的框架和今天 Zara Zhang 讲的「上手机会」那条是同一条线。

Josh Woodward(Google Labs):对软件的需求会爆表。

English

Josh Woodward (Google Labs product lead): 'The demand for software is going to be off the charts.'

The Google Labs lens on the same Jevons-paradox point Karpathy made: when building software becomes effectively free, the constraint shifts from 'can we build it?' to 'should we build it?'. The cultural question — what to build, what not to, what becomes a public good — is now the open question. Woodward's framing pairs with Zara Zhang's onboarding insight in the same feed.
