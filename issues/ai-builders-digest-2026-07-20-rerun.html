Theme 01

Agentic commerce 的真正缺口：token theft 与信任 / The missing layer in agentic commerce: token theft and trust

MAD Podcast 再访 Stripe 数据与 AI 负责人 Emily Sands：agent 经济并不先卡在支付按钮，而是卡在 token 滥用、权限边界和谁来承担错误。

TM
The MAD Podcast
Matt Turck 主理的 AI 与数据播客
中文

Stripe 数据与 AI 负责人 Emily Sands 把 token theft 钉成 AI 圈最少被讨论的问题之一。骗子不必再偷钱或账号，直接盗用推理额度就能获利；AI 公司每六个新增注册里，可能就有一个属于这类滥用。

所以 agentic commerce 的底座不只是支付 API，还得补上身份、额度、撤销、审计，以及 agent 买错东西时究竟谁承担损失。

骗子发现，在 AI 时代甚至不用偷钱或凭据，偷 token 就够了。

AI 公司新增注册中，超过六分之一可能就是这类滥用。

English

Stripe Head of Data and AI Emily Sands says token theft is one of AI's most under-discussed problems. Fraudsters no longer need to steal money or credentials; they can steal inference, and more than one in six new signups at AI companies can be this kind of abuse.

Agentic commerce therefore needs more than a checkout API. It needs identity, spend limits, revocation, auditability, and a clear answer to who pays when an agent gets the purchase wrong.

Fraudsters have figured out that in AI, you actually don't really need to steal money or credentials. You can just steal tokens.

More than one in six signups at AI companies are this kind of abuse.

English

The next step is not merely an agent buying on a person's behalf. Sands expects agents to run businesses: buying inputs, selling outputs, and optimizing for profit. That shift breaks per-seat pricing because the economic actor may have no human seat at all.

Her twelve-month horizon is a market where agents hold budgets and make repeated commercial decisions. The hard product problem is trust: users care about overspending, wrong purchases, and whether they can stop the agent.

It's Emily has an agent who's tasked with running a business, and that includes buying some things and selling some things and making some profits.

That'll be the world that I would like to be talking about twelve months from now.

成本下降，不会让 AI 支出下降 / Cheaper AI expands demand

Levie 与 Amjad 从两侧讲同一件事：token 越便宜，使用面越大；但消费软件的付费习惯仍决定谁能真正收钱。

AL
Aaron Levie
Box CEO
@levie
中文

Aaron Levie 的判断很直接：AI 越便宜，总支出反而越高。token 成本下降会放开更多写代码、安全审查、大数据处理和常驻 agent 的场景。

这也解释了开源模型为什么仍能养出大规模 infra 生意：大多数人不会在本地跑前沿模型，模型降价最终扩大的还是托管推理需求。

AI 越便宜，消耗量就会越大。

任何降低 token 成本的变化，都会推高推理需求。

English

Aaron Levie argues that cheaper AI will increase total AI spend, not shrink it. Lower token costs unlock more coding, security review, data processing, and always-on agents.

This also explains why open-source AI can support large infrastructure businesses: most users will not run frontier-class models locally, so lower model prices expand hosted inference demand.

When you make AI cheaper, it will get consumed more.

Anything that lowers the cost of tokens will drive up inference demand.

That makes giant consumer subscriptions rare outside Netflix- or Spotify-like categories. For AI products, capability alone is not enough; the product must attach itself to an existing consumer budget or create an obvious new habit.

Software is typically bought by their companies.

Outside of Netflix, Spotify, etc it's hard to think of huge consumer subscription businesses.

Theme 03

真实世界才是 AI diffusion 的限速器 / Reality rate-limits AI diffusion

代码能快速采用 AI，因为反馈闭环几乎全在电脑里；药物、销售、合同和工业流程都得等现实世界回答。

AL
Aaron Levie
Box CEO
@levie
中文

Levie 说 AI diffusion 最终会被现实世界限速。代码采用得快，是因为一个人能在电脑里生成、测试、运行大量代码，不必等待外部机构配合。

药物、销售、合同和工业设计则不同：实验、交易对手、监管和物理失败共同决定周期。所以真正的机会在 applied AI，把 workflow 围绕这些反馈回路重新设计，而不只是吐出一个模型答案。

想出点子从来不是最难的，难的是现实世界多快给你答案。

多数场景里，只有模型输出远远不够。

English

Levie says AI diffusion is ultimately rate-limited by contact with the real world. Coding moved quickly because one person can generate, test, and deploy nearly unlimited code without waiting on external institutions.

Life sciences, sales, contracts, and industrial design are different: experiments, counterparties, regulation, and physical failure determine the cycle time. The opportunity is therefore in applied AI systems that redesign workflows around those feedback loops, not in raw model output alone.

Coming up with ideas was never the hard part. The hard part is how fast reality answers them.

Model outputs alone are not enough in most cases.

文件要长寿，软件可以一次性 / Durable files, disposable software

Garry Tan 选 Markdown 当长期底座，Zhang Rui 则把临时 UI 和一次性分析页当可丢弃工具；两者并不矛盾。

GT
Garry Tan
Y Combinator 总裁
@garrytan
中文

Garry Tan 认为，正因为 intelligence stack 变化太快，Markdown 才是更耐久的数据格式。纯文本能跨过工具更替，还能阅读、diff 和迁移。

给 builder 的简单规则是：长期知识放在无聊而稳定的文件里，agent 和界面随时可以换。上层变化越快，稳定底座越值钱。

Markdown 文件具有普适性，甚至能存续千年。

当 intelligence stack 以极端速度变化时，它是非常合适的数据格式。

English

Garry Tan argues that Markdown is a resilient data format precisely because the intelligence stack is changing so quickly. Plain text survives tool churn and remains readable, diffable, and portable.

The practical builder rule is to keep durable knowledge in boring files and let agents or interfaces change around it. The faster the stack moves, the more valuable the stable substrate becomes.

Markdown files are universal and will survive millennia.

They are a fantastic data format when the intelligence stack is in flux at this extreme pace.

当开源模型只落后 frontier 一小步，单边 gatekeeping 可能让自己的公司失去工具，却拦不住其他生态。

AL
Aaron Levie
Box CEO
@levie
中文

Levie 认为，强大的开源模型已经改写安全账本。如果其他生态仍能使用接近 frontier 的开放替代品，单方面限制本国公司获取能力，反而会让它们竞争力更弱、防御能力更差。

这不等于不要监管，而是政策必须承认开放模型比预期更强、传播更广；单边 gatekeeping 已经不能保证控制力。

通过 gatekeeping 限制 frontier 模型能力，反而会让自己更不安全、更缺乏竞争力。

AI 监管的整套计算方式必须随之改变。

English

Levie argues that strong open-source models change the security calculus. If capable open alternatives remain available elsewhere, restricting domestic access to frontier capabilities can make local companies less competitive and less able to defend themselves.

The claim is not that regulation disappears. It is that policy must now account for models that are much stronger and more globally available than expected; unilateral gatekeeping no longer guarantees control.

You make yourself less secure and competitive by gatekeeping access to frontier model capabilities.

The calculus of what you do about AI regulation simply must change.
