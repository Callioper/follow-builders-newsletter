Theme 01

Boris Cherny 立 5-archetype 框架 / Cherny Lays the 5-Archetype Map for Future PM Roles

§1 是 6-29 整张 issue 的 load-bearing thesis——Boris Cherny 周日把未来 PM / 团队角色拆成 Prototyper / Builder / Sweeper / Grower / Maintainer 五种 archetype,标签按「做什么」分,不是按 job function 分。

BC
Boris Cherny
Salesforce AI 工程负责人
@bcherny
中文

Boris Cherny 周日把他在 Claude Code 团队里看到的未来角色拆成五种 archetype:Prototyper(出新点子,大多不 ship)、Builder(把点子推到 production)、Sweeper(清理、unship、优化)、Grower(对已 ship 的产品往 PMF 推)、Maintainer(守住一个成熟系统的安全 / 稳定 / 速度 / 效率)。

关键不是新名词,而是 archetype 不挂在 job function 上。设计师可以是 Prototyper,工程师可以是 Sweeper,PM 可以是 Grower。标签标的是你做什么,不是你的 title。Cherny 接着说:健康的团队是 1+2+3 / 2+3+4+5 / 3+4+5+2 这几种 mix,看产品处于 pre-PMF / 已找到 PMF / 强 PMF 的哪一阶段。

这个框架会被引用一阵子,因为它把「工程 vs PM vs DS vs 设计」那堵墙直接拆掉,换成一把按产品阶段分桶的 taxonomy。大部分「未来的工作」essay 都停在 vibe 层,这一篇周一就能上手用。

Cherny 把未来 PM 角色拆成五类:Prototyper / Builder / Sweeper / Grower / Maintainer——按「做什么」分,而不是按「title」分;健康的团队是这五类在不同产品阶段的 mix。

English

Boris Cherny, on a Sunday, lays out a five-archetype map for the roles he sees crystallizing inside the Claude Code team: Prototyper (churns out ideas, most don't ship), Builder (turns prototype to production), Sweeper (cleans up, unships, optimizes), Grower (iterates a shipped product toward PMF), Maintainer (owns a mature, secure, fast, efficient system).

The interesting bit is that the archetypes are not tied to job function. A designer can be a Prototyper, an engineer can be a Sweeper, a PM can be a Grower. The labels are about what you DO, not what your title says. Cherny adds that a healthy team is a MIX — pre-PMF needs 1+2+3, growing-PMF needs 2+3+4+some 5, strong-PMF needs 3+4+5+some 2.

The frame is going to be cited for a while because it dissolves the old 'eng vs PM vs DS vs design' wall and replaces it with a five-bucket taxonomy keyed to product stage, not org chart. Most 'future of work' essays stay at the level of vibes; this one is specific enough to use on Monday.

1. Prototyper: comes up with brand new ideas; churns out many ideas, most of which don't ship

2. Builder: quickly turns a prototype/idea into production-grade product/infra

3. Sweeper: cleans up the UI, simplifies the code and system, unships, optimizes performance

4. Grower: takes a product that has been built and iterates on it to improve Product-Market Fit

5. Maintainer: owns a mature system to make it secure, reliable, fast, and efficient as it scales

Maybe product roles of the future will look more like this, and less like the domain-specific roles of today?

Open-source mythos = 监管 baseline 被翻转 / Open-Source Mythos Is the New Regulatory Baseline

§3 是 regulatory 那一面:Levie 把 mythos 级别 open cyber 模型立成监管 baseline——「赌中国追不上」是 bad bet,要么 gate 自家模型(单边输),要么永远在 frontier 推下一波架构。

AL
Aaron Levie
Box CEO
@levie
中文

Aaron Levie 写了一篇:cybersecurity 这一层很快就会出现 mythos 级别、开放、任何人都能用的模型。副产品是 alternative tech stacks 会从美国的栈里分走更多经济价值和 control。

政策逻辑就接上了。如果先进模型无论如何都会开放,那么限制你自己不放出来,既不会让你更安全,也不会让你战略上更划算——只会让你单边吃亏。现在大多数 AI 监管框架暗地里都在赌中国追不上;但所有现有证据都说他们能追上、也正在追,而且「追上」在他们那里的优先级高得不能再高。

Levie 收尾是两条路:要么给自家最强的模型上 gate(等于跟自己未来的自己赛跑、还会输),要么保证自己永远站在 frontier、推下一波架构。没有第三种能站住的位置。

Levie 把 mythos 级别 open cyber 模型立成监管 baseline——「赌中国追不上」是 bad bet,要么给自家模型上 gate(单边输),要么永远站在 frontier 推下一波架构,没有中间态。

English

Aaron Levie, on what should be obvious to everyone in AI: mythos-level cybersecurity models will be open and available to anyone within a window. The byproduct is alternative tech stacks that drive economic value and control away from the US's stack.

The policy logic follows. If advanced models are going to be open regardless, then refusing to release your own doesn't make you more secure or strategically better off — it just asymmetrically disadvantages you. Most current regulatory framing is implicitly betting that China can't catch up; all current evidence suggests they can and are, and the strategic priority of doing so is about as high as it gets.

Levie closes with the only two real options: gate your best models (which means losing the race against your own future self), or work to always be at the frontier and driving the next architecture. There is no middle position that holds.

It should be 100% obvious that there will soon be mythos level models on cyber security that are open and available to anyone. As a byproduct of this, alternative tech stacks will emerge that also drive more economic value and control away from the US's tech stack.

If advanced models will become open and available regardless, then by not allowing the release of models you're neither more secure nor better off strategically. So much of the regulatory approach to AI has to assume China can't catch up, when all current evidence suggests they can and are.

So your options are either to create gates around your best models, which means you're asymmetrically disadvantaging yourself, or you work to ensure you're always at the frontier and driving the future architectures of AI.

English

Matt Turck's MAD Podcast lands Stephen Balaban, Lambda co-founder and CTO, for a deep cut on the physical layer that everything else in AI runs on. The thesis up front: 「It's pretty clear that we have an amazing system that can take in money and output software. The people that are the naysayers, you're gonna throw these GPUs out in five years, are completely wrong.」

Balaban's frame: cloud compute is NOT a commodity service. It is a vertically-integrated stack spanning land entitlement, construction, HPC design, software, virtualization, cloud services on top — which is why the biggest companies in the world are all in this business. The 「GPU is a commodity」 narrative that floated a few years ago was misunderstood.

He makes a non-obvious point about used hardware: a 2023 H100 can actually be more expensive to lease today than when it was bought, because the demand curve is that steep. The most direct connection to today's thread: physical-layer work, which used to be the opposite of a builder role, is now the constraint that determines who gets to be a Builder at all.
