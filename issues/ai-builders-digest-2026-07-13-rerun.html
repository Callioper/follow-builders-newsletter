Taylor 对 AI-compute 受众抛出的最尖锐一句:LLM 的成本大头是电和散热,不是 silicon——所以任何能把能源成本曲线压 10 倍的反应堆,几乎是顺带把智能本身的成本压了 10 倍。

他对监管环境的描述非常直白:「多年来第一次,能源 R&D 的监管通道是打开的」。没有明说但显然的受益方,是把牌照路径和硬件迭代节奏配在一起的那家公司——Valar 赌自己能成为那家公司。

Valar 真正在押的更深一层是:AI 的下一个瓶颈不是下一颗 chip,是下一吉瓦。如果 nuclear 是解锁下一代 training run 和 inference fleet 的 input,那么能源丰裕的赢家在结构上就坐在「模型最优者」的上游。

我们的反应堆更接近于被制造出来而不是被建造出来,而且它们极其安全。我们认为这两件事的组合会让我们把 nuclear fission 规模化,把人类的能源成本压到现在的十分之一。

为什么多年来第一次,能源 R&D 的监管通道是打开的。

能源是我们超级 techno-industrial 未来的唯一 input。

English

Taylor's strongest single line for the AI-compute audience: most of the cost of running an LLM is electricity and cooling, not silicon — so any reactor that bends the energy cost curve 10x is, almost by accident, bending the cost of intelligence itself.

He is explicit about the regulatory environment: 'an open regulatory environment for progress in energy R&D for the first time in years.' The unstated beneficiary is whoever pairs the licensing pathway with hardware iteration — Valar is betting they can be that company.

The deeper bet Valar is making is that the bottleneck for AI isn't the next chip — it's the next gigawatt. If nuclear is the input that unlocks the next generation of training runs and inference fleets, then the winner of energy abundance is structurally upstream of whoever ships the best model.

We have nuclear reactors that are more manufactured than constructed, and they're extremely safe. And we think the combination of these two things is going to allow us to scale nuclear fission and make energy 10 times cheaper for humanity.

Why there's an open regulatory environment for progress in energy R and D for the first time in years.

Energy is the only input to our hyper techno industrial future.
