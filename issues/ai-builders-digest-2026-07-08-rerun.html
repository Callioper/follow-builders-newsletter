Anthropic 把 containment 立成工程一等公民 / Anthropic Elevates Containment to First-Class Engineering

今天 Anthropic 工程博客发了可能是迄今最完整的一份公开稿,讲清楚他们怎么在 claude.ai / Claude Code / Claude Cowork 三个产品上「围住」Claude。同一个哲学——先把环境层做硬,再回到 model 层去调行为——落到三种完全不同的 containment 架构上。三条线最值得带走:approval fatigue 是真的(93% 的权限弹窗被秒过),他们自己写的中间件反复出事(allowlist proxy、trust 之前的配置解析),而攻击者最爱 egress(两起最严重的事故都是数据沿着「被允许的路径」走出去的)。

AE
Anthropic Engineering
Anthropic 官方工程博客
中文

Anthropic Engineering 发了可能是迄今最完整的一份公开稿,讲清楚他们怎么在 claude.ai、Claude Code、Claude Cowork 三个产品上「围住」Claude。同一个哲学——先把环境层做硬,再回到 model 层去调行为,不是去调 agent 本身——落到三种完全不同的 containment 架构上。

三件事值得带走:(1)approval fatigue 是真的——93% 的权限弹窗用户都会点同意,human-in-the-loop 跑着跑着就成 human-in-the-confirm;(2)他们自己写的中间件(allowlist proxy、trust 之前的配置解析)反复出事,而 gVisor、seccomp、hypervisor 这些标准件一直稳;(3)攻击者最爱 egress——两起最严重的事故,数据都是沿着被允许的路径走出去的(员工被钓鱼、第三方 allowlist 泄漏)。

一年前,我们会直接拒绝把「足以打到内部服务」的权限交给 Claude。今天这种权限已经常规化,Anthropic 自己的开发效率也是被它撑起来的。部署风险有两部分:失败概率,失败后果。前者随着模型训练和防护在持续下降;后者——理论上的 blast radius——只会随能力变强、可访问范围变大而继续扩大。

第一种思路是 human-in-the-loop,让用户在每一轮监督 agent。早期的 Claude Code 就是这么做的:每次写、跑命令、走网络,都弹窗让用户点同意。理论上能 work,但我们发现这条路实际守不住——遥测显示用户点同意的比例是 93%,弹窗越频繁,用户越不认真。

English

Anthropic Engineering publishes the most detailed public account of how the company contains Claude across claude.ai, Claude Code, and Claude Cowork. Three product surfaces, three containment architectures, and a clear philosophy: design the environment first, then steer behavior at the model layer. The blast radius is what gets capped, not the agent itself.

Three core lessons: (1) approval fatigue is real — 93% of permission prompts get approved, so the human-in-the-loop stops being a loop; (2) the custom pieces (their allowlist proxy, their pre-trust parser) keep breaking, while the standard primitives (gVisor, seccomp, hypervisor) hold; (3) egress is the attacker's favorite — two of the worst incidents were data leaving through permitted paths (employee phish, third-party allowlist disclosure).

Twelve months ago, we'd have rejected out of hand the idea of granting Claude access sufficient to take down an internal Anthropic service. Today that level of access is routine, and Anthropic developers are more productive for it. The risk of these deployments has two components: how likely a failure is, and how much damage one could do. Progress on safeguards and model training has steadily driven down the first; the second—the theoretical blast radius—only grows as capabilities and access expand.

The first is to supervise the agent's behavior via a human-in-the-loop. Claude Code previously protected against agents taking unintended actions by asking users for permission at each turn. Theoretically that works, but we've found the approach to be fallible. Our telemetry showed users approved roughly 93% of permission prompts. The more approvals a user sees, the less attention they pay to each.

Aaron Levie 刚从一打企业 IT leader 的会里出来,把这七条共性摆出来:(1)operating model——agent 要打破组织里的孤岛;(2)数据碎片化是 agent 准确率的天花板;(3)数据护城河正在替代分发护城河;(4)token 不是对的指标,业务结果才是;(5)多模型路由是迟早的事;(6)AI 人才是最稀缺的;(7)最好的 use case 是改工作方式,不是单纯加速。

这份清单正好是 Anthropic 那篇 containment 文章的「企业侧前置条件」——Levie 在列「公司还没解决的、containment 假设已经被解决的」那些事。数据碎片化、多模型路由这两条,直接对应 containment 必须在环境层挡住的攻击面。

刚和一打企业 IT leader 开完 agent 的会,共同议题至少七条: * 多数讨论卡在 operating model——大公司的组织历来是按 silo 切的,但 agent 只有绑在流程上才跑得动,流程又经常横跨 silo。问题变成:谁来管这些 agent?怎么部署、怎么被采用? * 数据碎片化是普遍痛点。只要数据继续散在各处、格式不统一,或者该看到的人和 agent 看不到,企业就只能接受 agent 给的答案要么不准确、要么不符合自己的业务口径。结构化数据(产品指标、收入)和非结构化数据(产品路线图、客户合同)都中招。

English

Aaron Levie, fresh off meetings with a couple dozen enterprise IT leaders, lays out the seven recurring themes: (1) operating model — agents have to break org silos; (2) data fragmentation blocks agent accuracy; (3) data moats replace distribution moats; (4) tokens are not the right metric, business outcomes are; (5) multi-model routing is coming; (6) AI talent is the bottleneck; (7) best use cases change the work, not just speed it up.

This is a complementary read to the Anthropic containment blog — Levie is naming the enterprise-side blockers the containment blog assumes are solved. The data-fragmentation bullet and the multi-model-routing bullet are the two that map directly onto what containment has to handle.

Just coming off of meetings with a couple dozen enterprise IT leaders discussing AI agents. Here are a few of the common themes that stand out: * Lots of conversation that you have to solve an operating model challenge to get the full benefits of AI. Most companies have orgs that have always operated in siloes; but agents are most effectively when they are tied to a process, which often cuts across these siloes. So the big question is how do you start to deploy centrally managed agents that can work across organizational boundaries. Who manages these agents? How do they get deployed and adopted? * Data fragmentation remains a major issue for most organizations. As long as data remains highly fragmented and not in standard formats, or data is not available to the right people and agents, enterprises are dealing with issues around being able to get answers from agents that are accurate or that conform to their business practices. This cuts across both systems with structured data (product metrics or revenue figures) and unstructured data (product roadmap or customer contracts).

Training Data sits down with Zipline co-founder Keller and systems/safety lead Eric. Zipline has flown 140 million miles with zero incidents, started in 2011, pivoted in 2014, and refuses to be called a drone company — they are an 'automated logistics system for Earth, approximating teleportation.' The founding insight: in Rwanda, doctors and lab techs didn't want better drones, they wanted a 24/7 service because 'people get sick twenty-four seven.'

The 'we are not a drone company' line is the most direct echo of today's containment theme. Just as Anthropic's blog argues containment is what you ship, not the model itself, Zipline's product is the logistics system, not the drone. Both companies learned the same lesson — the customer doesn't care about the technology operating behind the curtain, they care about the experience.

The closer is the most builder-coded: 'we gotta get good at building stuff again — vehicles, hardware, infrastructure. The anti-suicide nets on the Golden Gate Bridge cost more to install than it cost our grandparents to build the bridge.' A US competitiveness / build-thing argument that lands as a podcast closer.

I remember being in Rwanda early days and going out and meeting with some of the doctors and lab techs that we were serving and asking for them, like, you know, how's it going? We've always wanted to be an extremely customer obsessed company, and the reality is none of our customers care at all about drones.
