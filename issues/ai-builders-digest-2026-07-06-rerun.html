Noam Brown 在 No Priors 上讲 test-time compute 怎样改写评测与 safety / Noam Brown on No Priors: Test-Time Compute Reshapes Benchmarks and Safety

benchmarks 没控制 test-time compute 是错;capability 现在是预算的函数,policy 框架还没追上。

NP
No Priors
AI 与创业对话播客
中文

Noam Brown 在 No Priors 上讲:每次新模型发布时附的那张 benchmark grid 数字呈现方式错了——它没有控制每道 benchmark 题实际用了多少 test-time compute。

5.5 在 paper 上看只比 5.4 好一点,但 5.5 只是 thinking 效率高很多;一旦把思考时间控制住,5.5 是大幅跳升。这和用户拿到手玩起来的实际感受对得上。

它就是一个模型在一个 benchmark 上的一个数字。你看 paper 上 5.5 和 5.4 之间的差——是有提升,但不是巨大提升。

在某些 benchmark 上只多了几个百分点。所以大家看了之后怀疑 5.5 真的更好。

我觉得它在 benchmark 上看不出大幅提升的原因是 benchmark 的呈现方式错了——它没有控制那道 benchmark 题实际用了多少 test-time compute。

结果是 5.5 的 thinking 效率就是高很多。如果跑 max 设置,5.4 想的时间要长得多。

English

Noam Brown on No Priors: the benchmark grid that gets published with every new model release is presenting the numbers in the wrong way — it isn't controlling for the amount of test-time compute used on each benchmark question.

5.5 looked only marginally better than 5.4 on paper. But 5.5 is just much more efficient with its thinking; once you control for thinking time, it's a substantial jump. That matches what people felt when they actually played with it.

It's like a single number for a model on a single benchmark. And if you look on paper at the difference between, like, five point five and five point four or other models, it wasn't it was an improvement, but it wasn't a huge improvement.

It's only a few percentage points on some benchmarks. So people looked at that, and they were skeptical that it was actually a better model.

I think the reason why it doesn't show up as so much better on the benchmarks is because the benchmarks are being presented in the wrong way. They're not controlling for the amount of test on compute that is being used on that benchmark question.

It turned out that 5.5 is just much more efficient with its thinking. If you run it at max settings, 5.4 is thinking for a lot longer.

Noam Brown 接着讲 safety evaluation:目前的 responsible scaling policy 和 preparedness framework 问的是错的问题。它们把模型 capability 当成一个固定数字去量,但 capability 现在是一个关于你往里砸多少钱的函数。

给 frontier 模型 $10、$10,000、$10,000,000 三档预算,跑出来的是三条不同的 capability 曲线。现有政策框架没回答「应该按哪档预算来评估」,而这正是 safety 真正要问的问题。

那些 preparedness framework 和 responsible scaling policy,它们其实没把 test-time compute 的量算进去。

它们只问:这个模型的 capability 是什么?问题是我们现在活在一个模型 capability 是关于你往里砸多少钱的函数的世界里。

给 $10,000 预算它能做的事远多于给 $10 预算。给 $10,000,000 预算还能做更多。

应该按哪档预算来评估这些模型?现行政策根本没回答这个问题。

English

Noam Brown on safety evaluations: current responsible scaling policies and preparedness frameworks are asking the wrong question. They measure a model's capability as if it were a fixed number, but capability is now a function of how much money you throw at it.

Give a frontier model a $10 budget or a $10,000 budget or a $10,000,000 budget and you get three different capability curves. The policy frameworks don't address at what budget you should evaluate these models, which is exactly the question that matters for safety.

The precarious frameworks and responsible scaling policies, they don't really account for the amount of test time compute.

They just say, okay. Well, what's the capability of the model? The problem is we're in a world now where the capability of the model is a function of how much money you put into it, basically.

If you give it a budget of $10,000, it can do a lot more than what it can do with a budget of $10. Give it a budget of $10,000,000, it can do even more.

At what budget should you evaluate these models? The policies that exist today don't really address that question.
