Theme 01

Post-transformer 与 Agent 的两难 / Post-Transformer Whispers and the Agent Inflection

Transformer 论文共同作者 Lucas Kaiser 上 Unsupervised Learning,自己承认「reasoning 不够通往真正的泛化」,post-transformer 实验室在偷偷成立;同时他自己每天用 Codex 写代码、10x 提速,承认「agent 是过去二十年他工作方式的最大变化」。

UL
Unsupervised Learning
Redpoint 出品的 AI 投资与技术播客
中文

Lucas Kaiser——原 transformer 论文的共同作者,前 Google Brain,现 OpenAI——给了一个相当坦诚的「我们现在到底在哪」判断。一句话版本:现在的组合(transformer + chain of thought + RL + 一个 shell 加工具)已经惊人,但「感觉还是不太像我们」——它要花一万亿个 token 才能学到人几乎免费就拿下的概念。

他把这个归类为一种「vibe」,但 vibe 后面是真事——好几个 post-transformer 实验室正在认真追这件事。小模型 TRM、HRM 加上一点 recurrence,已经能在 Sudoku 这类结构化问题上跑得很顺;悬念是这套配方能不能搬到语言。他的态度是「不知道答案,但 case for something else 确实在变强」,即便 transformer 自己也在一直追。

到 agent 这一面,Kaiser 讲得很直白:这是他过去二十年做 ML 研究,工作方式最大的一次变化。他给了一个具体数字——重做一篇老论文,以前要三周跑到能跑的状态,Codex 两。他也讲清楚 agent 的翻车模式:它会自己发明一个根本没人要的 aux loss,你还是要对「机器学习层面到底在跑什么」有完整的心智模型。

Lucas Kaiser:当前 transformer + 推理 + RL + shell 加工具,已经惊人;但「感觉还是不太像我们」——它要花一万亿 token 才学到人能直接拿下的概念。

现在 case for「还有别的东西」正在变强,即便 transformer 自己也在追;小模型 TRM、HRM 加点 recurrence 在结构化问题上跑得很顺,但能不能搬过来还不知道。

重做一篇老论文以前要三周,Codex 两天——10x 提速是常态;但 agent 会自作主张发明一个 aux loss,人还是要对「机器学习层面在跑什么」有完整心智模型。

English

Lucas Kaiser — co-author of the original transformer paper, ex-Google Brain, now at OpenAI — gives one of the most candid reads of where the field actually stands. His one-line summary: the current setup (transformer + chain of thought + RL + a shell and tools) is incredible, but it doesn't quite feel like us. It needs a trillion tokens to learn concepts that a human picks up almost for free.

He frames this as a 'feeling' that several post-transformer labs are now chasing in earnest — small models like TRM and HRM already do well on structured problems like Sudoku when you bolt on a bit of recurrence, and the open question is whether that recipe generalizes to language. He doesn't pretend to know the answer; he just notes the case for 'something else' has gotten stronger even as transformers have kept catching up.

On the agent side, Kaiser is blunt: this is the biggest change to how he works as an ML researcher in twenty years. He gives a concrete number — re-implementing an old paper used to take him three weeks, and Codex got him to a runnable state in two days. He is also clear about the failure mode: the agent will invent an auxiliary loss that nobody asked for, and you still have to hold the full mental model of what is actually running.

If we we take the current transformers with reasoning, right, and and and agents and they have access to a shell and and and stuff, they can do amazing things. Right? It's incredible how far we've gotten, like, two years ago even, not to mention before transformers. I would have never believed that, you know, you just take this next word predictor, give it then chain of thought and a r l dot and tools, and that it will, I know every day spend hours talking to Codex, in my case, or other people just and it works. Right?

There is this feeling that we have not exploited all that is there in machine learning. Obviously, the exciting feeling is that maybe if we find out what's out, it could make what we have even more amazing. Maybe not. Maybe maybe it vanishes when you have that much data. Who knows?

I I I can fairly well quantify it because I I tried recently just on the on a private machine to reproduce a bunch of papers, like old papers that they were was always quite interested in? Even some of my papers that I lost code for. At least one of them I tried to reproduce before, and I knew it took me about three weeks to get to a runnable state. Theme 02

模型路由走到 Phase 3 / Model Routing Is the New Product Layer

Madhu Guru 在 Gemini 时期观察到企业用模型的三个阶段,Aaron Levie 用同一套话讲 token 成本上涨会把「路由能力」变成应用层的新护城河——两个独立观察拼成同一张图。

M
Madhusudhan
AI 工程师 / 开源贡献者
@realmadhuguru
中文

Madhu Guru(前 Google Gemini / Veo)给企业用模型的过程画了三阶段。Phase 1(2024):默认「the it model」,不管什么任务都用 GPT。Phase 2(2025 年初):矫枉过正,没练出 eval 内功就开始找最小最便宜的模型,结果反而烧时间、还把上线速度拖慢。Phase 3:eval 基础设施和模型多样性都到位,最成熟的 AI-native 创业公司开始把产品拆成 sub-agent,按子任务路由到不同模型——最难的推理给 Claude,最轻的给 Gemini Flash-Lite 或开源模型。

企业跟 AI-native builder 的节奏,通常差 6 到 9 个月。

路由难就难在要把每个任务映射到对的模型——这需要按自家产品任务做 benchmark,在质量/成本之间做权衡。Madhu 在 Gemini 看到的三个阶段:Phase 1 默认 GPT,Phase 2 矫枉过正没 eval 内功,Phase 3 才出现真正的「按子任务路由」。

English

Madhu Guru (ex-Google Gemini / Veo) lays out the three phases he watched enterprises go through on model usage. Phase 1 (2024): default to the 'it' model — everyone used GPT regardless of task. Phase 2 (early 2025): over-correct, hunting for the smallest/cheapest model without eval muscle, and ship slower for it. Phase 3: the eval infrastructure and model diversity catch up, and the most sophisticated AI-native startups break the product into sub-agents and route each task to the right model — hardest reasoning to Claude, simplest to Gemini Flash-Lite or open weights.

Enterprises, as usual, follow the AI-native builders 6–9 months later.

Routing to models is genuinely hard. It means mapping each task to the right model - which requires benchmarking models against your product's specific tasks and dialing in the quality/cost trade-off. And there is an opportunity in that difficulty. Here is the progression I saw with enterprises while on Gemini. Phase 1 (2024): Default to the "it" model. Everybody used GPT regardless of task. Phase 2 (early 2025): Over-optimize. Teams over-corrected, looking for the smallest/cheapest model for their task, but did not have evals sophisticated enough to map tasks to models. They ended up burning cycles and shipping slower. Phase 3: Nuanced routing.

中文

Box CEO Aaron Levie 从另一个角度讲同一件事:token 成本突然变成每一家企业都在谈的热点——这对整个 AI 行业是利好,说明系统被用到了一个没人预料过的规模。但同时也给应用层催出了一个新的分水岭:模型路由。

结构性的判断是:token 占了工作流成本的更大份额之后,企业一定会要求每一块钱都花在「最适合这个任务」的模型上。高端任务(写代码、法律、金融分析、医疗)依然吃 frontier 模型的份额;但拆出来的子任务,可以分给更便宜的模型(开源权重或大厂的低端线),整体把单次结果的成本打下来。

按 Levie 的判断,差异化会在应用层:比别人更懂你所在 domain 的工作流,有最好的 eval,能按文档类型、按法律问题去配模型。商业模型和客户的「钱花得值」绑在一起的公司,会卡住这一层。

Token 成本突然变成企业最热的话题——对 AI 整体是利好(说明用到了没被预料过的规模),同时给应用层催出新的分水岭:模型路由。高端任务依然吃 frontier,子任务分给便宜模型,关键是谁有最好的 eval 和 workflow 理解。

English

Aaron Levie (Box CEO) pushes the same point from a different angle: token costs are suddenly one of the hottest topics in every enterprise conversation, and that is bullish for AI overall — it means these systems are running at a scale nobody predicted. But it also creates a new axis of differentiation at the applied AI layer: model routing.

The structural bet: as tokens take a larger share of any given workflow's cost, companies will insist that every dollar go to the most efficient model for the specific job. Frontier intelligence still wins on the high end (coding, legal, financial analysis, healthcare), but you peel off individual tasks to lower-cost models — open-weights or major labs — to deliver a cheaper end outcome.

The differentiation, per Levie, lives in the applied layer: knowing the workflows of your domain better than anyone else, having the best evals, the best ability to mix and match models for specific document types and specific legal questions. The companies whose business model is aligned to the customer's financial outcome will own this layer.

Token costs are becoming one of the hottest topics for any enterprise I talk with right now. It's very bullish for AI in general because it means these systems are being used at a scale that wasn't contemplated before. It also gives way to another form of differentiation that will emerge for the applied AI layer, which is model routing. […] Frontier intelligence will always be relevant at the high end of tasks, like coding, legal and financial analysis, healthcare, and more. And dollars spent here will only go up over time. But, equally, you can peel off individual tasks to lower cost models (whether they're from open weights vendors or the major labs) and deliver a more efficient end outcome.
